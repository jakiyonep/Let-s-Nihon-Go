How dogs bark in your language? In Japanese, わんわん(wan-wan). As you already know Japanese has an abundant onomatopoeia thanks to Manga. Manga doesn't have sound so manga creators have to express sounds by letters or pictures. This is how we developed bunch of onomatopoeia. So if you want to master onomatopoeia, read Japanese original manga, but not ジョジョ(Jojo's Bizzare Adventure). Anyway, this time we are going to touch on some of basic onomatopoeia in Japanese that we often use. [TOC] ##彼は今 イライラしているから、そっとしておいた方がいいよ He is ira-ira now, so you should leave him alone. イライラする - get irritated
そんなことでイライラするな
Don't be ira-ira over such as tiny thing.
なんか分からんけど、イライラする
I don't know why but I'm getting ira-ira. ##コロナで外出れないから、家でずっとゴロゴロしてる I can't go out because of the Coronavirus, I spend the whole day doing goro-goro at home. ゴロゴロする - lie around, sleep When you say ゴロゴロする, it usually means that you do nothing, or chill out at home, so people see you as a lazy person.
休みの日は家でゴロゴロしたい
I just want to goro-goro on a day-off
ゴロゴロしてないで宿題やれ！
Stop goro-goroing, and do your homework! ##何も食べてないからお腹ぺこぺこ I haven't eaten so I'm peko-peko ペコペコ-starving
